Frequently asked questions

DailyPrep is available to download for free. Check the App Store or Google Play listing for the current pricing details and any available subscription options.

DailyPrep covers a broad range of software engineering topics including backend development, frontend, databases, algorithms, data structures, system design, and more. You select the categories and topics that match your role and interview target, and the app serves questions tailored to those selections.

Each day you get exactly three tasks: a technical question (from your selected stack), a behavioral question, and a knowledge-check swipe session using cards from topics you've previously practiced. Try to answer each question yourself before revealing the hint or full answer — that's where the real learning happens.

Yes — you can update your categories and topics at any time from the Statistics screen inside the app. Your progress for previous topics is preserved, and new content will load for any newly selected areas.

Go to the Statistics screen and tap the Reset option. This will clear all your progress and XP, and the app will show the initial setup screen the next time you open it. This is useful if you want a completely fresh start or to change your focus area entirely.

DailyPrep collects optional, anonymous crash reports and usage analytics (which screens you visit, when you complete tasks). No account or personal information is required. You can opt out during initial setup or at any time via the Statistics / Reset option. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

DailyPrep is available for iOS (iPhone) on the App Store and for Android on Google Play.

Content is loaded from the server when you first set up the app and during updates. Once loaded, practice sessions can generally be completed without an active internet connection.
